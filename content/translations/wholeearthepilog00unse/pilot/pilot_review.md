## Where This Loop Worked

- The source pack made leaf, printed page, scan URL, and OCR source explicit, which prevented mixing OCR and reader summaries.
- The context and glossary passes helped keep repeated technical terms consistent across pages, especially Bateson/Wilden/Rappaport/Hardin vocabulary.
- The self-critique step caught places where the translation could have silently guessed, especially the leaf 5 diagram.
- Compressing bibliographic/order data kept the translation focused without losing substantive review or excerpt content.

## Where It Remains Unstable

- Dense multi-column pages still need image-backed verification, especially figures, captions, vertical labels, and low-contrast small type.
- The OCR source is sufficient for prose but unreliable for page furniture, diagrams, and broken technical words.
- Some book-title translations are provisional. A larger workflow should decide whether to use established Chinese translations where they exist or keep literal working titles.
- Philosophical/control-theory terms need a shared glossary before batch translation, otherwise later pages may drift.

## Recommendation

Proceed to the next small pilot batch, but include a required high-resolution crop step for diagrams and tables. For ordinary prose-heavy entries, the loop is viable; for figure-heavy pages, the review status should remain `revise` or `needs_human_scan` until image text is resolved.
